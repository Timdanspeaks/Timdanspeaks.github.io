Tim's Life Story

Some personal details have been changed for personal reasons. 

Early Life 

Tim was born more than 17 years ago to Christian parents. Tim grew up at Faith Community Bible Church, where he was taught in the ways of Christian Conservatism, professed faith at 5, and was baptized at 7. His parents Gragg and Karla made it a point that Tim knew the Bible well, and implemented scripture passage and outline memorization while encouraging personal Bible reading. They also homeschooled Tim.   

Young Conscious Life

Tim liked math, air hockey, baking with Grandma, Legos, video games, playing baseball, football, soccer, and other sports with his brothers, and eating potato chips. He would often go to Dollar Tree and buy little trinkets and bibelots such as capsule foam "creatures" and plastic pretend sets. Later on, Tim also enjoyed giving birthday gifts to certain female friends and his best other friends. 

Teenage Life

Tim went from spending all his time on video games and school to yo-yoing, woodworking, crocheting, painting, wood burning, knitting, watercolor, hand balancing, drawing, piano playing, gymnastics, gardening, singing, aliquoting, debating, Bible studying, landscaping, teaching...and more things. Tim has worked a couple jobs, but now enjoys online classes at Bob Jones University and CLEP tests through Modern States as well as playing disc golf every now and then.      

Tim's Spiritual Life Story

Sometime between May 2012 and April 2013

Tim's Profession of Faith

When Tim was 5 years old, he professed faith in Jesus Christ as his Savior shortly after the viewing of a VeggieTales episode. Tim does not currently remember this day at all and does not think he understood what Jesus being Lord meant (Romans 10:9). 

Tim's Baptism

Being that Tim gave a satisfactory testimony to the elder board at FCBC (church), Tim was baptized when he was 7 years old and used that day as proof of his salvation for the next 5 years. Tim had yet to know what Jesus being Lord really meant.   

April 2015

Summer 2020

Yoyo Gift

Many years have passed and Tim is now a tween. The Speaks have left FCBC and are currently doing online church at Grace Community Church. Tim and Juan decided separately that they were going to end their video game addiction and do better things with their time. Then the Speaks were gifted yoyos by friends and Tim got pretty interested. In a month, Tim bought a yoyo kit, was watching hundreds of yoyo videos weekly, and getting really good at yoyoing through hours of practice. Karla (Mom) was happy now that Tim had abandoned video games and had moved onto to better things.    

Video Game Kid 

At speech club, Tim (who owned 3 yoyos now) was approached by a kid who wanted to discuss strategical decisions and tactics in a Pokemon video game Tim had never heard of. Tim, seeing himself as a recently freed video game addict, told the kid that it would be better if he took the 4-5 hours he played video games and read the Bible instead so he could get to know God.   

October 5th, 2020

October 5th, 2020

Conversation with Self

Tim was doing Latin in a back room of the debate building, which gave him opportunity to think about other things. He thought about what he told the video game kid, and said to himself, "Self, you told him to give up his fun hobby(video games) to read his Bible, but you wouldn't give up your fun hobby(yoyoing) to read your Bible!"

Self said back, "Yeah, but yoyoing is way better for you than video games, and anyway, I'm too invested in it to waste my skills, time, and money!"

Tim agreed. 

Heart Change

Tim decided to read one chapter of the Bible that morning since he was behind on his one-chapter-a-day plan. He happened to read Romans 2, which includes this verse: "you, therefore, who teach another, do you not teach yourself? You who preach that one shall not steal, do you steal?"

Tim was shocked - he could not escape the reality that God was calling him to Himself. Thus, Tim decided and resolved that he would do nothing in the future that was not glorifying to God, and from that moment, the Holy Spirit made the Bible that Tim had neglected to read (because of disinterest and major interest in other things) interesting and exciting to read. At the same time, all the things that Tim loved most of the world became uninteresting and rather dull - even yoyoing.  

October 6th, 2020

March 5th, 2021

Spiritual Life Beginnings

The commands of the Bible now became necessities for Tim, and he has made some small progress in mortifying the deeds of old Tim. On this day Tim finished reading through the Bible, having started in Genesis on October 6th. Tim continues to love the Word of God, even when it does not make immediate sense, and tries to obey God's commands while trusting in Him. At this point the Speaks are at a third church, which is Pacific Hope Church. 

Barabbas Road Church

The Speaks left Pacific Hope Church, and decided to try Barabbas Road Church. We had been to ~15 different churches while looking for a home church, and thought that this one would be no different. Yet the church was singing songs with good theology, preaching from Leviticus chapter by chapter (sermon length = ~1 hour 15 mins), and seemed to agree with the Bible on all points we could see.

September 25th, 2022

Believer's Baptism 

Being convicted that I had seen no fruit of belief before October 6th, 2020, I decided to be baptized - this time as a believer who was trying to be obedient to the Word of God because of the Lordship of Christ. I was baptized at Barabbas Road Church on September 25th, 2022. 

Me Now

I am more aware of my need for Christ than I have ever been, and do not profess to have any righteousness of my own. I can say that I have been blessed in the study of Scripture, and that making the Bible your compass for life will help you infinitely more than you can help yourself. I am hoping to make my profession the study of Scripture, as I hope to become a pastor. I am thankful for what God has given me, and will go wherever He leads me.
